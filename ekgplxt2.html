Inferior lead region

Inferior leads (II, III, aVF) view the inferior surface of the left ventricle, often supplied by the right coronary artery.

ST elevation in these leads suggests an inferior myocardial infarction; reciprocal ST depression is often seen in I and aVL.

Lead group Surface region Typical artery
II, III, aVF Inferior wall Right coronary artery
V3–V4 Anterior wall Left anterior descending
I, aVL, V5–V6 Lateral wall Left circumflex / diagonal
V1–V2 Septal region Proximal LAD
  • Use the inferior leads first in chest pain to screen for an inferior ST‑elevation MI.
  • Always scan opposite leads for reciprocal changes to confirm the territory.
