üü¢ Extrinsic Pathway (Tissue Factor)

Tissue Factor (TF) + Factor VII ‚Üí VIIa
Trigger: Vascular injury exposes Tissue Factor
Function: Binds Factor VII, activating the extrinsic pathway

Factor X ‚Üí Xa (Converges to Common Pathway)
Activated by: TF-VIIa complex

Activated by: Factor Xa with cofactor Va
Function: Central enzyme in coagulation

Vitamin K dependent: Yes
‚Üì

Converted by: Thrombin
Function: Forms the mesh framework of the clot
Structure: Initially forms soft clot, then cross-linked by Factor XIIIa
‚Üì
Factor XIII ‚Üí XIIIa (Fibrin Stabilizing Factor)
Activated by: Thrombin
Function: Cross-links fibrin polymers to form stable clot
Result: Mechanically strong, insoluble clot

Pharmacology of Anticoagulants

üîµ Warfarin (Coumadin) - Vitamin K Antagonist

Inhibits vitamin K epoxide reductase, preventing regeneration of reduced vitamin K needed for synthesis of factors II, VII, IX, X and proteins C & S.

Mechanism
Blocks vitamin K dependent clotting factors
Onset
3-5 days (must deplete existing factors)
Monitoring
INR (target usually 2-3)
Reversal
Vitamin K, Fresh Frozen Plasma, PCC
Key Interactions
Many drug & food interactions (leafy greens)
Indications
DVT, PE, A-fib, mechanical heart valves

üíâ Heparin & Low Molecular Weight Heparin (LMWH)

Binds to antithrombin III, increasing its activity 1000-fold. Antithrombin III then inactivates thrombin (IIa) and Factor Xa.

Mechanism
Activates antithrombin III ‚Üí inactivates IIa & Xa
Onset
Immediate (IV/SC)
Monitoring
aPTT for UFH; anti-Xa for LMWH
Reversal
Protamine sulfate (binds heparin)
Adverse Effects
HIT (Heparin-Induced Thrombocytopenia)
LMWH Advantage
More predictable, less monitoring needed

üî¥ Direct Oral Anticoagulants (DOACs)

Direct Thrombin Inhibitors: Dabigatran
Factor Xa Inhibitors: Rivaroxaban, Apixaban, Edoxaban

Mechanism
Directly inhibit thrombin or Factor Xa
Onset
1-4 hours (rapid)
Monitoring
Generally not required
Reversal
Idarucizumab (dabigatran), Andexanet alfa (Xa inhibitors)
Advantages
Fixed dosing, fewer interactions, no INR monitoring
Contraindications
Severe renal impairment, mechanical valves

üü° Antiplatelet Agents

While not true anticoagulants, these prevent platelet aggregation.

Aspirin
Irreversibly inhibits COX-1 ‚Üí ‚Üì TXA2 production
Clopidogrel
P2Y12 inhibitor ‚Üí blocks ADP-induced aggregation
GP IIb/IIIa Inhibitors
Block final common pathway of platelet aggregation

For acute DVT treatment, initial therapy typically involves LMWH (enoxaparin) or fondaparinux for rapid anticoagulation, bridged to warfarin OR starting a DOAC directly. DOACs are increasingly preferred for their convenience and safety profile.

Coagulation Disorders

ü©∏ Hemophilia A (Factor VIII Deficiency)

Inheritance: X-linked recessive

Clinical Features: Spontaneous bleeding into joints (hemarthrosis), muscles, soft tissues

Lab Findings: Prolonged aPTT, normal PT, normal platelets

Treatment: Recombinant Factor VIII replacement, Desmopressin (mild cases), Emicizumab (prophylaxis)

ü©∏ Hemophilia B (Factor IX Deficiency - Christmas Disease)

Inheritance: X-linked recessive

Clinical Features: Similar to Hemophilia A but often milder

Lab Findings: Prolonged aPTT, normal PT

Treatment: Recombinant Factor IX replacement

ü©∏ von Willebrand Disease (vWD)

Inheritance: Autosomal dominant (most common inherited bleeding disorder)

Pathophysiology: Deficiency or dysfunction of von Willebrand Factor (vWF) ‚Üí impaired platelet adhesion and Factor VIII stabilization

Clinical Features: Mucosal bleeding (epistaxis, menorrhagia), easy bruising, prolonged bleeding after procedures

Lab Findings: ‚Üì vWF antigen, ‚Üì vWF activity, may have ‚Üì Factor VIII, prolonged bleeding time

Treatment: Desmopressin (releases vWF stores), vWF concentrates

‚ö° Disseminated Intravascular Coagulation (DIC)

Pathophysiology: Widespread activation of coagulation ‚Üí consumption of platelets & clotting factors ‚Üí paradoxical bleeding

Causes: Sepsis, trauma, malignancy, obstetric complications, transfusion reactions

Clinical Features: Both bleeding (from factor depletion) and thrombosis (microthrombi)

Lab Findings: ‚Üì platelets, ‚Üì fibrinogen, ‚Üë PT/aPTT, ‚Üë D-dimer, schistocytes on blood smear

Treatment: Treat underlying cause, supportive care with platelets/FFP/cryoprecipitate as needed

üîµ Thrombophilias (Hypercoagulable States)

Factor V Leiden: Most common inherited thrombophilia; resistance to activated protein C

Prothrombin G20210A: Mutation causing elevated prothrombin levels

Protein C/S Deficiency: Loss of natural anticoagulants ‚Üí increased clotting

Antithrombin Deficiency: Severe thrombophilia; antithrombin inhibits multiple factors

Antiphospholipid Syndrome: Antibodies against phospholipids ‚Üí recurrent thrombosis, pregnancy loss
